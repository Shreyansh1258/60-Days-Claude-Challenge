# Day 7: Understanding Claude Models & Reasoning Effort

## 📚 Overview

Claude offers multiple models optimized for different tasks. Choosing the right model and reasoning effort level can improve output quality, response speed, and overall efficiency.

---

## 🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will understand:

* The purpose of Claude Haiku, Sonnet, and Opus models.
* When to use each model.
* How reasoning effort affects responses.
* Best practices for selecting the right model and reasoning level.

---

## 🤖 Claude Models

### 1. Haiku

**Best for:**

* Quick summaries
* Text rewriting
* Simple Q&A
* Data extraction
* High-volume tasks

**Advantages:**

* Fastest response times
* Lowest cost
* Excellent for straightforward requests

---

### 2. Sonnet

**Best for:**

* Coding assistance
* Content creation
* Research synthesis
* Business analysis
* Everyday AI workflows

**Advantages:**

* Strong reasoning abilities
* Fast performance
* Best balance between speed and intelligence

---

### 3. Opus

**Best for:**

* Deep research
* Strategic planning
* Complex problem solving
* Advanced reasoning
* Long-form analysis

**Advantages:**

* Most capable model
* Deepest reasoning
* Best for high-complexity tasks

---

## 💡 Key Takeaways

✅ Use **Haiku** for speed.

✅ Use **Sonnet** for most tasks.

✅ Use **Opus** for deep reasoning and complex work.

✅ Increase reasoning effort when accuracy and analysis matter more than response speed.

---

## 🎉 Conclusion

Understanding Claude's models and reasoning effort levels allows you to optimize performance, cost, and response quality. In most situations, **Sonnet with Standard reasoning** provides the best balance, while **Opus with High or Max reasoning** is ideal for advanced analytical work.
