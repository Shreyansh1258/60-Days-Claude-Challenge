Nutrition Sources
Data references, methodology, and scientific basis for NutriScope's calculations.
Data Sources
1
ICMR-NIN Nutritive Value of Indian Foods (2017)
Primary reference for all Indian foods in the database. Indian Council of Medical Research – National Institute of Nutrition, Hyderabad.
2
USDA FoodData Central (2023)
Secondary reference for global foods and cross-validation of micronutrient values. United States Department of Agriculture.
3
Mifflin-St Jeor Equation (1990)
Used for basal metabolic rate estimation. Validated as the most accurate predictive equation for healthy adults (Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics).
4
ICMR Recommended Dietary Allowances (2020)
Source for all micronutrient Daily Reference Intakes including iron, calcium, B12, folate, zinc, and vitamins for Indian adult population.
5
WHO/FAO Protein and Amino Acid Requirements (2007)
Basis for protein target calculations (1.6g/kg body weight for active individuals).
Methodology
⚡
Energy Target Calculation
TDEE = BMR × Physical Activity Level (PAL). BMR via Mifflin-St Jeor. Goal adjustments: −300 kcal (loss), +200 kcal (gain).
🥩
Macro Split
Protein: 1.6g/kg BW. Fat: 25% of TDEE ÷ 9. Carbs: remaining kcal ÷ 4. Adjusted proportionally for weight goals.
🔬
Nutrient Values
All values are per 100g edible portion (cooked weight for grains/legumes, raw weight for fruits/vegetables unless noted). Values are averages and may vary by variety, cooking method, and preparation.
⚠️
Limitations
Bioavailability of nutrients (e.g. non-haem iron, phytate-bound calcium) is not modelled. Individual absorption varies. This tool does not account for drug-nutrient interactions or clinical conditions.
