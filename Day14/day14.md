## Overall Risk Assessment

**Risk Level:** Medium Risk

The company appears to be a legitimate startup with credible founders and institutional clients. However, there are several factors that require careful evaluation before accepting an offer.

---

## Key Red Flags

### 1. Long Internship Duration

* Mandatory internship period of **12 months**.
* Monthly stipend of **₹20,000**.
* This structure is commonly used by startups to obtain full-time development work at a significantly lower cost.

### 2. Below-Market Compensation

* Post-internship compensation is capped at **₹6 LPA**.
* For a developer with one year of practical React and Node.js experience, this compensation may be below market standards in Delhi-NCR.

### 3. Potential Scope Creep

* The job description mentions opportunities to learn about investing and business operations.
* This may indicate that responsibilities could extend beyond software development tasks.

### 4. Startup Environment

* Smaller startups often have less structured processes.
* Learning opportunities can be high, but expectations and workloads may also be unpredictable.

---

## Positive Signals

### 1. Legitimate Company Presence

* Company appears to have real operations.
* Institutional clients are mentioned.
* Founders reportedly have IIT/IIM backgrounds.

### 2. No Employment Bond

* No long-term service agreement or financial bond.
* Employees can leave if the role does not meet expectations.

### 3. Full-Stack Exposure

* Opportunity to work with modern technologies such as:

  * React
  * Node.js
  * Full-stack web development

### 4. Startup Learning Opportunity

* Exposure to product development and business operations.
* Potential for rapid skill growth due to broader responsibilities.

## Final Verdict

The opportunity is **not an obvious scam** and appears to come from a legitimate startup. However, the **12-month internship at ₹20,000/month** is the most significant concern and should be discussed thoroughly during the interview process.

The role may be worthwhile if:

* You prioritize learning and hands-on experience.
* The company provides meaningful mentorship.
* There is a clear path to salary revision based on performance.

Proceed with caution and seek clarification regarding compensation progression, evaluation criteria, and expected responsibilities.
---

## Key Learnings

* Long internships should be evaluated carefully, especially when compensation is significantly below market rates.
* Startup opportunities can provide rapid learning but may involve less structure and broader responsibilities.
* Compensation after internship completion should be discussed early in the hiring process.
* Employment bonds significantly increase risk; the absence of a bond is a positive sign.
* Researching founders, clients, and company history helps distinguish legitimate startups from potentially risky opportunities.
