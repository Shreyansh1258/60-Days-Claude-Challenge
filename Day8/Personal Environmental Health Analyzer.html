Executive Summary: Analysis of 8 major Indian cities (June 8, 2026) reveals a wide air-quality spectrum. Kolkata leads as the most polluted with AQI 136, driven by high humidity trapping particulates, while Mumbai and Bangalore are the cleanest metros with AQI ~56–63. Delhi sits at AQI 117 (Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups) — a summer improvement from its winter peaks above 300, yet still 2.3× above the 'Moderate' threshold. The average AQI across analyzed cities stands at 89.5. Water quality in Delhi averages 580–650 ppm TDS — above WHO's recommended 500 ppm — indicating hard water with implications for hair and skin health. Most surprising: Hyderabad's AQI (80) outperforms New Delhi on a summer day, despite comparable industrial activity — largely due to its Deccan Plateau geography enabling better wind dispersal.
