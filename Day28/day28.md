# Day 28 – Hospital Admission Readiness Simulator using Claude

## Objective

Build an interactive **Hospital Admission Readiness Simulator** using Claude that demonstrates the complete hospital admission workflow involving providers, physicians, insurance companies, utilization review teams, nursing staff, and administrative personnel.

---

## Key Learnings

### Admission Readiness

* Learned how hospitals evaluate admission readiness before patient admission.
* Understood the importance of completing all operational requirements.

### Healthcare Operations

* Explored how different hospital departments coordinate during admissions.
* Learned the interaction between providers, physicians, nursing staff, utilization review, and insurance teams.

### Prior Authorization

* Understood how insurance approval affects admission decisions.
* Learned how pending requests and appeals delay admissions.

### Documentation Management

* Realized that incomplete documentation increases administrative risk.
* Learned that complete clinical documentation improves approval chances.

### Risk Management

Reduced several operational risks including:

* Documentation Risk
* Insurance Risk
* Clinical Risk
* Bed Availability Risk

### Governance Snapshot

Observed governance metrics including:

* Compliance Status
* Readiness Score
* Administrative Risk
* Clinical Risk
* Insurance Status
* Final Admission Recommendation

### Interactive Simulation

Built and tested a browser-based workflow simulation generated entirely with Claude.

---

## Outcome

Successfully created and tested a Hospital Admission Readiness Simulator that models real-world hospital admission workflows, improves understanding of healthcare operations, and demonstrates how administrative, insurance, documentation, and clinical factors influence admission decisions.
